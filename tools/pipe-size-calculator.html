How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the required flow rate in gallons per minute (GPM), or enter total fixture units to auto-calculate GPM.
  2. Select the maximum velocity (5 ft/s for copper, 8 ft/s for PEX/CPVC is typical).
  3. Choose your pipe material.
  4. Click "Calculate Pipe Size" to get the recommended nominal pipe size.

The Formula

Q (ft³/s) = GPM ÷ 448.83
A (ft²) = Q ÷ Velocity
Inside Diameter (in) = √(4 × A ÷ π) × 12

Flow rate from fixture units uses the Hunter's Curve approximation. Standard nominal pipe sizes: ½″, ¾″, 1″, 1¼″, 1½″, 2″, 2½″, 3″. Always verify with local plumbing code.
