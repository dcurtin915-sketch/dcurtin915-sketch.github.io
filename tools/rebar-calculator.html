How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter the length and width of your concrete slab or footing.
  2. Select the bar spacing (12″ O.C. is common for residential slabs).
  3. Choose the rebar size (#4 is most common for residential).
  4. Set the stock bar length (standard is 20 ft).
  5. Choose grid (both directions) or one-way layout.
  6. Click "Calculate Rebar" for your material list.

The Formula

Bars in length dir. = ⌊Width ÷ (Spacing/12)⌋ + 1
Bars in width dir. = ⌊Length ÷ (Spacing/12)⌋ + 1
Total Linear Ft = (Bars_L × Length) + (Bars_W × Width)
Stock Bars = ⌈Total Linear Ft ÷ Bar Length⌉
Weight = Total Linear Ft × Weight per Ft
Lap Splice = 40 × Bar Diameter (ACI 318)

Bar weights: #3 = 0.376, #4 = 0.668, #5 = 1.043, #6 = 1.502, #7 = 2.044, #8 = 2.670 lb/ft. Lap splice uses ACI 318 minimum of 40 bar diameters for tension splices.
