How to Use This Calculator

  1. Enter your monthly kWh usage (check your electric bill) or your monthly bill amount.
  2. Select your area's peak sun hours — this is the average daily equivalent hours of full sunlight.
  3. Choose the panel wattage — 400W is the current residential standard.
  4. Set your offset target — 100% means fully offsetting your usage.
  5. Click "Calculate Solar System" for your results.

The Formula

Daily kWh Needed = (Monthly kWh × Offset%) ÷ 30
System Size (kW) = Daily kWh ÷ Peak Sun Hours ÷ 0.80
Number of Panels = System kW × 1000 ÷ Panel Wattage

The 0.80 factor accounts for real-world system losses (inverter efficiency, wiring, temperature, shading, soiling). Typical losses range 15–25%. Each panel needs approximately 17.5 sq ft of roof space (standard 65" × 39" panel).
